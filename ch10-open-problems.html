10. Open Problems & Future Directions

No foundational theory is complete without a clear articulation of what remains unresolved. This chapter collects open problems, conjectures, and possible extensions of ToE, ranging from mathematical questions about the entropic field equations to physical questions about empirical signatures and testable predictions. It outlines research pathways for further development and invites readers—especially theorists and mathematically inclined physicists—to treat ToE as a living program rather than a finished edifice.

Every major monograph ends with: unresolved questions, research pathways, conjectures, potential extensions. This is where I define the frontier.
