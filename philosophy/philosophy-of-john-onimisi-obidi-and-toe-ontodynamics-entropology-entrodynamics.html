On the Philosophical System of John Onimisi Obidi:
From Ontodynamics to Entropology and Entrodynamics in the Theory of Entropicity (ToE)

John Onimisi Obidi’s philosophical system becomes fully coherent only when it is understood as a unified worldview constructed around three mutually reinforcing pillars: Ontodynamics, Entropology, and Entrodynamics, all grounded in the Theory of Entropicity (ToE).

His work does not merely propose a new physical theory. It advances a new metaphysics of reality, a new epistemology of knowing, and a new dynamics of becoming, each rooted in the primacy of the entropic field. What emerges is not an isolated scientific framework but a comprehensive philosophical architecture in which physics, ontology, and epistemology are inseparably intertwined.

This exposition presents a full-length articulation of Obidi’s philosophical system as a monograph-level synthesis, integrating the scope of his conceptual innovations, scientific motivations, and foundational principles.

Foundational Inversion in the Theory of Entropicity

Obidi’s philosophy begins with a radical inversion of the traditional foundations of physics. Instead of treating particles, forces, or spacetime geometry as the bedrock of existence, he asserts that entropy itself is the fundamental field: a dynamic, causal, and universal substrate from which all physical structures emerge.

In classical physics and relativity, geometry is primary and matter is placed within it. In Obidi’s framework, entropic curvature precedes geometry. Spacetime, matter, classicality, and even temporal ordering arise as consequences of entropic evolution rather than as presupposed primitives.

The Theory of Entropicity is therefore not an extension of existing physics, but a reconstruction of physical reality from first principles.

Ontodynamics: The Metaphysics of Entropic Being

Ontodynamics is Obidi’s term for the metaphysical foundation of the Theory of Entropicity. It redefines existence, causality, and transformation strictly in entropic terms.

Entropy as the Fundamental Field

Entropy, in Obidi’s system, is not a measure of disorder. It is the causal substrate of physical reality. The entropic field, denoted as \( F_E \), is the primitive entity from which the following emerge:

  • spacetime geometry
  • gravitational behavior
  • quantum phenomena
  • classical outcomes
  • distinguishability
  • measurement irreversibility

The universe, in this view, is not constructed from particles embedded in spacetime. It is constructed from entropic gradients that give rise to particles, fields, and spacetime as emergent structures.

Being and Becoming Reinterpreted

Ontodynamics reframes classical philosophical categories. Being is understood as the persistence of entropic gradients within finite boundaries. A system exists insofar as its entropic configuration remains sufficiently stable to be distinguishable.

Becoming is the irreversible redistribution of entropy across the entropic field. Change is not accidental or optional; it is the necessary unfolding of entropic evolution. Existence itself is therefore dynamic, provisional, and continuously negotiated.

“God or Nature Cannot Be Rushed” (G/NCBR): The Logic of Distinguishability

A central principle of Obidi’s philosophy is that reality unfolds at a finite, non-negotiable entropic rate. No physical structure can manifest before its entropic curvature reaches the threshold required for distinguishability.

This principle is formalized in the No-Rush Theorem (NRT), which asserts that:

  • no process can evolve faster than the entropic time-limit constant
  • no classical outcome can stabilize instantaneously
  • no measurement can occur without entropic irreversibility

The universe cannot be rushed because distinguishability itself requires entropic maturation.

Dethroning the Observer: Reality as Pre-Computed Entropic Dynamics

Obidi’s philosophy decisively de-centers the observer. Unlike interpretations of quantum mechanics that grant the observer a privileged metaphysical role, ToE treats observers as ordinary subsystems embedded within the entropic field.

Observers are local, entropically constrained systems subject to the same physical limitations as all other structures. Reality is not created by observation; it is pre-computed by entropic dynamics. Measurement is the entropic stabilization of distinguishability, not an act of consciousness.

This restores objectivity without invoking hidden variables or observer-dependent collapse.

Entropology: The Physics of Knowing

Entropology replaces traditional epistemology with a physically grounded theory of knowing. It studies how systems come to register, stabilize, and preserve information through entropic interaction.

In this framework, consciousness is an entropic negotiation between subsystems, perception is the stabilization of entropic gradients into classical outcomes, and knowledge is the accumulation of entropic structure. Knowing is not a mental abstraction but a physical process governed by the entropic field.

Entrodynamics: The Dynamics of Entropic Evolution

Entrodynamics concerns the evolution of the entropic field and the manner in which this evolution generates physical law. It encompasses finite-rate propagation of entropic influence, emergence of spacetime from entropic curvature, stabilization of classicality, and the irreversibility of measurement.

Central to Entrodynamics is the No-Go Theorem (NGT), which states that any proposed law \( L \) that cannot coexist with the entropic field \( F_E \) collapses into contradiction:

\[ L \wedge F_E = \bot \]

The entropic field thus becomes the ultimate arbiter of physical admissibility.

The Universe as a Living Accounting Mechanism

Obidi characterizes the universe as a self-organizing continuum that redistributes entropy, maximizes flow, minimizes constraint, and evolves toward greater distinguishability. Human beings are temporary but not trivial participants in this process—entropic but not meaningless.

Meaning arises not from permanence, but from participation in the universal entropic unfolding.

A Comprehensive Philosophical Synthesis

Obidi’s philosophy forms a unified system:

Domain Role
Ontodynamics Explains what exists as entropic gradients
Entropology Explains how systems know through entropic negotiation
Entrodynamics Explains how reality evolves via finite-rate entropic propagation

Together, these constitute a complete metaphysics, epistemology, and dynamics grounded in the primacy of entropy. This is why the Theory of Entropicity appears simultaneously as a new physics and a new philosophy.

A natural continuation of this exposition is the formal mathematical development of these principles through the Obidi Action and the Obidi Field Equations (OFE).
